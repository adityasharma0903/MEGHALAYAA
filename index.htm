Welcome to Meghalaya – The Jewel of Northeast India

Discover the Magic of Meghalaya

Meghalaya, one of the Seven Sister States of northeast India, invites you to explore its enchanting landscapes and vibrant culture. Known as the "Abode of Clouds," this pristine destination offers breathtaking views, lush green valleys, and a rich tapestry of experiences waiting for you.

Majestic Geography

Experience the awe-inspiring mountainous terrain of Meghalaya, adorned with verdant valleys and highland plateaus. Its diverse geography is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike, offering a perfect backdrop for exploration and relaxation.

The Name Behind the Beauty

Named by Shiba P. Chatterjee, a distinguished geography professor at the University of Calcutta, Meghalaya’s name reflects its majestic natural beauty. Explore a land where every corner tells a story.

Enchanting Rivers

Garo Hills Region: Navigate through serene rivers like Ganol, Daring, and Simsang, each offering unique landscapes and tranquil experiences.

Central and Eastern Plateau: Discover the captivating rivers such as Umiam and Umngot, known for their scenic beauty and adventure opportunities.

Southern Khasi Hills: Marvel at rivers that carve deep gorges and create stunning waterfalls, providing the perfect setting for nature enthusiasts.

Elevation and Peaks

Elevation Range: From 150 m (490 ft) to 1,961 m (6,434 ft), Meghalaya's varied elevation offers diverse experiences and landscapes.

Notable Peaks: Climb to Shillong Peak (1,961 m) for panoramic views or explore Nokrek Peak (1,515 m) in the Garo Hills for a rewarding adventure.

Climate – The Wettest Wonder

Rainfall: With annual rainfall reaching up to 12,000 mm (470 in), Meghalaya is renowned as the wettest place on Earth. Embrace the refreshing climate and enjoy the lush, green environment.

Temperature: From the cooler temperatures of Shillong, rarely above 28°C (82°F), to the warmer climates of the Garo Hills, Meghalaya offers a pleasant climate year-round.

Records: Witness the world record-setting rainfall in Sohra (Cherrapunji) and Mawsynram, the champions of monsoon precipitation.

Cultural Tapestry

Languages Spoken: Meghalaya's rich cultural diversity is reflected in its languages. English is the official language, with Khasi, Garo, Pnar, and other local languages enriching the cultural experience.

Embark on an unforgettable journey to Meghalaya, where adventure meets tranquility, and nature’s wonders await you at every turn.
